ℹ️ Equipment & System Guide

Welcome to the Predictive Maintenance guide. This page explains the machines we monitor, what the sensors do, and what signs of trouble the system looks for. It's designed to be easy to understand for operators, engineers, and management alike.

🏭 The Machine

We use a CNC milling machine — a highly precise, computer-controlled cutting machine on the factory floor.

Every time it runs, we record the conditions around it and how it's performing. The machine makes parts in three quality levels:

  • Low (L): Basic, everyday parts. Faster making, less force (~60% of runs).
  • Medium (M): Standard parts. Balanced speed and force (~30% of runs).
  • High (H): Precision parts. Slower, stronger cuts for parts that need extra care (~10% of runs).

⚠️ What Can Go Wrong

Our system acts as an early-warning radar. It checks for five different types of breakdowns at once to warn you before they happen:

  • Tool Wear: The cutting tool has been used too long and is too dull to cut safely.
  • Overheating: The machine can't cool down fast enough and temperature spikes.
  • Power Failure: The machine is spinning and pushing harder than is mechanically safe.
  • Overstrain: A worn-out tool combined with high force pushes the machine past its physical limits.
  • Random: Rare, unpredictable problems that happen occasionally.

📡 The Sensors Explained

We rely on five main sensors to tell us how the machine is feeling. Here's how to read them:

Sensor What it measures Normal Range Why it matters
🌡️ Room Temperature
Unit: Kelvin (°K)		 How warm or cool the air is around the machine. 295 – 305 °K If the room is too hot, the machine can overheat and break down faster.
🔥 Machine Temperature
Unit: Kelvin (°K)		 How hot the machine itself gets while working. 306 – 314 °K When it gets too hot inside, it means things are rubbing too hard—a clear sign of trouble.
🔄 Spin Speed
Unit: RPM		 How fast the main spinning part (spindle) is turning. 1,200 – 2,900 RPM Spinning too fast or slow compared to normal means the motor or belt might be wearing out.
💪 Twisting Force (Torque)
Unit: Newton-meters (Nm)		 How hard the machine has to push to cut the material. 3 – 77 Nm A sudden jump in force usually means the tool is stuck or the material is unexpectedly tough.
⏱️ Tool Usage Counter
Unit: Minutes		 How many minutes the current cutting tool has been actively running. 0 – 253 mins Old tools get dull and can snap. Replacing them on time prevents major breakdowns and damage.
